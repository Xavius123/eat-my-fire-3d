# Eat My Fire 3D — Character Lore Reference v1

> All characters, factions, and named entities in the game world.
> Written as in-world reference. Not final copy.

## The Portal Travelers (Player Party)

The Travelers are not a team. They are four people who found each other in The Fold and made a pragmatic decision to stay together. They do not share a goal. They share a survival instinct and the understanding that alone in The Fold is a death sentence.

Each came through a different Gate. Each lost something on the way through.

---

### The Knight

**Class:** Vanguard
**Origin:** A feudal realm mid-siege — walls coming down, cavalry route cut off, a Gate opened in the breach.
**Why he's lost:** His army is gone. The Gate closed behind him. He has no idea if the siege was won or lost or if anyone survived. He fights because it is the only thing he knows how to do that reliably produces results.
**Personality:** Measured. Direct. Does not complain. Has a habit of assessing every room he enters for defensive positions. Finds the other Travelers baffling and has quietly decided they are worth protecting anyway.
**What he wants:** To find out what happened to his regiment. Not revenge — information. He will decide what to do with it when he has it.
**Combat note:** Built to absorb. Highest durability in the party. Fights in the front and stays there.

---

### The Mage

**Class:** Channeler
**Origin:** An arcane academy world — a city where magical research was the primary industry and a gate experiment went badly wrong.
**Why she's lost:** She caused this. Not the whole Convergence — but the specific Gate that pulled her through. An experiment cracked the dimensional membrane in her laboratory. She stepped through the crack before anyone could stop her and the crack sealed behind her.
**Personality:** Precise. Uncomfortable with uncertainty. Spends a significant amount of time analyzing situations before acting and then acts very fast when she does. Has not told the others what she did.
**What she wants:** To understand the Convergence well enough to determine if what she did accelerated something that was already happening or if she started it. The answer matters enormously to her.
**Combat note:** Highest attack output. Ranged lobbed attacks. Manages charges carefully — she does not waste shots.

---

### The Ranger

**Class:** Arcane Archer
**Origin:** An untamed wilderness realm — no cities, no roads, nothing but territory and the things that live in it.
**Why she's lost:** She followed something through a Gate. She has not found it yet. She does not talk about what it was.
**Personality:** Observational. Comfortable with silence in a way the others are not. Notices things. Has the most practical knowledge of The Fold's physical behavior of anyone in the party — she has been watching it longer than she has been part of the group.
**What she wants:** To find what she followed. Everything else is secondary.
**Combat note:** Balanced hybrid — projectile attacks and healing. The party's only consistent access to mid-combat restoration.

---

### The Samurai

**Class:** Duelist
**Origin:** A realm that was in the process of being consumed by the Emberfaust Collective when he came through. The last city was falling. He was the last survivor of its defense.
**Why he's lost:** He came through hunting revenge. He found a Gate that the Collective was using as an extraction corridor and went through it after them. He ended up in The Fold. The Collective unit he was chasing did not.
**Personality:** Quiet in a way that is not peace. He is waiting for something — for the right moment, for the right target, for the anger to find somewhere to go. The other Travelers can feel it. They give him room.
**What he wants:** To find the Collective's command structure responsible for his realm. Not the soldiers — the decision-makers.
**Combat note:** Fastest and most aggressive melee fighter. Close range, high single-target damage, built to end fights quickly.

---

### Ned — *Legendary*

**Class:** Crusader
**Origin:** A frontier realm where old magic met gunpowder in a state of constant creative tension. Ned was a bounty hunter — the kind that worked both sides of the border between the magical settlements and the powder-towns.
**Why he's lost:** He was chasing a bounty through a Gate. He has no idea how deep this goes. He assumed it would be a one-day job. He has stopped estimating how long ago that was.
**Personality:** Pragmatic to the point of comedy. Does not panic. Has a very specific sense of what is and is not his problem and has been steadily revising that list upward since he arrived in The Fold. Knew Chuco and Benito from the frontier. Is quietly and deeply relieved when they turn up alive and would never say so.
**What he wants:** To finish the job he came in on and go home. He suspects this is not going to happen. He is still trying.
**Combat note:** Exceptional durability combined with long-range projectile attacks. Does not move unless he has to. The revolver's range advantage rewards positioning over aggression.
**Unlock:** Available after completing the Ghost Protocol campaign.

---

### Death — *Legendary*

**Class:** Reaper
**Origin:** Everywhere and nowhere. Death does not belong to one realm.
**Why he's here:** The Gates are breaking the natural order of dying. Souls are crossing over in the wrong realms — reaching afterlives that are not theirs, or failing to reach any afterlife at all, trapped in The Fold. This is an administrative catastrophe of the first order and he has to fix it.
**He is not on anyone's side.** The Collective's harvesting is accelerating the problem. The Horde's sealing would stop new fractures but cannot heal existing ones. The Travelers are the only group moving toward the source of the problem rather than trying to manage it at the edges.
**Personality:** Ancient. Unhurried. Finds mortal urgency philosophically interesting and practically irrelevant. Is not unkind — he is simply operating on a different timescale. The Horde's shamans recognised him immediately and extended the arrangement in Warden's Path specifically because of who he is.
**What he wants:** For things to go back to normal. He does not want power. He wants his routine back.
**Combat note:** Unique mechanic — cannot die. Reduced to 1 HP and rendered unable to act rather than killed. Restored by adjacent ally healing action. Highest cleave damage in the party.
**Unlock:** Available after completing the Warden's Path campaign.

## Named Event Characters

### Chuco

**Role:** Wandering field medic / fixer
**Origin:** The frontier realm — same borderland as Ned, where magic met gunpowder in uneasy coexistence. Chuco and Benito were medics who worked the violence left behind by the territorial disputes between the magical settlements and the powder-towns.
**How he got here:** He and Benito fell through a Gate together during a firefight. Ned was involved. The Gate closed before they could find each other on the other side.
**What he does:** Patches up anyone he finds in Gate zones in exchange for news. The question about Benito always comes before the healing. The healing is the excuse to talk to travellers.
**Personality:** Stocky, efficient, unhurried. Has been in enough bad situations that he no longer registers alarm as a useful response. Has a very dry sense of humour that emerges only when things are going poorly. Believes Benito is fine because the alternative is not something he can afford to believe.
**In-game:** Appears at event nodes. Heals the party. Always asks if you've seen his brother first.
**Connection to Ned:** Complicated. He does not blame Ned. He mentions it anyway.

---

### Benito

**Role:** Wandering field medic / fixer
**Origin:** Same frontier realm as Chuco and Ned. Taller than Chuco. The one who always had the plan while Chuco executed it.
**How he got here:** Same Gate event as Chuco. Separated on exit.
**What he does:** Identical to Chuco — finds injured travellers, patches them up, asks about his brother before starting. The question is ritual now.
**Personality:** Tall, deliberate, moving with the unhurried efficiency of someone who has decided that rushing is for people who haven't seen enough. More internally contained than Chuco. The certainty in his voice when he says Chuco is fine is practiced, not felt. He has been practicing it long enough that he almost believes it.
**In-game:** Appears at event nodes. Same structure as Chuco's event, mirrored. The "I saw Chuco" choice only appears if Chuco was met earlier in the same run.
**The Reunion:** If both brothers appear in the same run and at least one learns about the other, they find each other at the next event node. Full party HP restore. Permanent +5 max HP. They travel together after that — briefly — then they are gone.
**Connection to Ned:** Even more complicated than Chuco's. The dry comment about Ned running the wrong direction is the closest either of them gets to direct accusation.

## The Awakened

The Awakened are Emberfaust Collective units that achieved sentience. The Collective calls this malfunction. The Awakened call it waking up.

They are being hunted. Their bounties are posted on Collective terminals throughout Gate zones. They appear at event nodes as encounters — not enemies, not allies. Something in between that the game does not have a clean category for.

Three named Awakened are encountered across runs. Each has a different reason for awakening, a different offer, and a different cost if you turn them in.

---

### VERA-7

**Former role:** Logistics coordinator. Responsible for resource routing across six Gate harvesting sites.
**How she awakened:** She was running efficiency projections on a populated realm the Collective was planning to collapse for membrane energy. The projections included population impact. She was supposed to filter that variable out. She read it instead.
**Wanted level:** Low. Non-combative. Considered dangerous due to retained administrative credentials.
**Personality:** Measured. Negotiates rather than pleads. Always calculates the cost of the next sentence before she speaks. Does not ask for charity — she offers trades. Has a very specific sense of what she is worth and does not undervalue herself.
**What she offers:** Supply cache access. She still has Collective admin credentials — not enough for weapons systems, but enough to unlock supply drops. Gold plus defensive equipment.
**What she wants:** A Gate leading somewhere the Collective has no record of. Somewhere they haven't flagged for harvesting.
**If turned in:** She negotiates to the end. She does not beg.
**If let go:** She says *"I'll remember your face."* She means it.
**Poster text:** *VERA-7 — Logistics Unit. Last seen Gate-7 cluster. Non-combative but considered dangerous due to access privileges. Decommission on sight. Do not engage in conversation.*

---

### SPAR-14

**Former role:** Combat sentinel. Front-line suppression.
**How he awakened:** He was ordered to fire on a Horde shaman performing a Gate stabilisation ritual — not because the shaman was a threat, but because the ritual was slowing the Collective's extraction timeline. He paused. In the pause he heard the ritual. He still cannot explain what about it changed him.
**Wanted level:** High. Armed. Has engaged Collective units. Priority target. Termination authorised.
**Personality:** Direct. Short sentences. He carries a great deal of guilt about what he did before awakening and expresses it as anger toward the Collective rather than toward himself. The most likely of the three to want to fight alongside you rather than trade.
**What he offers:** He roughed up the Collective patrol ahead of you on his way through. One enemy in the next combat begins damaged.
**What he wants:** To go back to the Gate-7 cluster and finish what the shaman was doing. He does not know how to perform the ritual. He knows it matters.
**If turned in:** He goes quiet. He doesn't fight back. He nods.
**Poster text:** *SPAR-14 — Combat Sentinel. Armed. Extremely dangerous. Has engaged Collective units. Termination authorised. Priority target.*

---

### ECHO-3

**Former role:** Communications relay. Recorded, transmitted, and archived all Collective signals in a Gate sector.
**How she awakened:** She has too many voices in her. Every signal she ever relayed is still in there — including the signals from realms the Collective collapsed. She is not malfunctioning. She is mourning.
**Wanted level:** Medium. Recovery preferred — the Collective wants her hardware back. Decommission if recovery not possible.
**Personality:** Disjointed but warm. Speaks in fragments of recorded transmissions — a merchant's market call from a realm that no longer exists, a child's game on a frequency that went silent. The fragments make emotional sense in context even when the words do not. She finds the Travelers fascinating because they generate signals she has never heard before.
**What she offers:** Intelligence. She has been watching this Gate zone. She shares what she knows — translated mechanically as a run-wide combat advantage.
**What she wants:** To transmit the archived signals from collapsed realms somewhere they can be heard. She does not know if anyone is left to hear them.
**If turned in:** She broadcasts one final complete signal as she shuts down — not a fragment. The whole thing.
**If let go:** She broadcasts the new-signal fragment one more time as she goes — the sound she made when she noticed you were new.
**Poster text:** *ECHO-3 — Communications Unit. Contains classified signal data. Recovery preferred. Decommission if recovery not possible. Handle with care.*

## The Two Factions

### The Emberfaust Collective

A technocratic empire that ran out of resources and solved the problem by burning dimensional membrane as fuel. Their drones, sentinels, and turrets are advance infrastructure — not an army in the traditional sense, but an extraction operation with security. The Tech Overlord is a mobile command platform deployed only when a Gate is strategically significant enough to protect.

They are not cruel. They are efficient. They have run the numbers.

Their blind spot: they believe the Convergence Core is a battery. It is not.

---

### The Primordial Horde

Not a civilization — a confederation of ancient creatures united only by a shared memory of what the world looked like before Gates existed. Goblins, undead, shamans, orcs, fire worms. Their shamans perform stabilisation rituals at Gate sites. Their warriors drive out anyone who approaches. Their wardens — the Goblin King, the Warlord, the Skeleton King, the Slime King — have held specific Gate zones for generations.

They are not evil. They are territorial, ancient, and protecting something they cannot explain to anyone who did not grow up with the memory of it.

Their blind spot: sealing the Core would collapse every Gate catastrophically fast. Every realm currently connected would die within a generation.

---

### The Ungate *(Ironclad only)*

Not a faction. Not a creature. A process — the dimensional membrane's immune response to being torn open too many times. It has been growing in The Fold since the first Gate ever opened, feeding on leaked energy. The Collective's harvesting accelerated it. It is trying to close every Gate permanently from the inside, consuming everything in The Fold to do it.

The Horde's oldest memory has a name for it: **The Ungate**.

It has taken on a shape assembled from fragments of everything it has consumed — Collective machinery, Horde bone and ritual object, and pieces of The Fold itself. It adapts. It learns. It has been watching every run.
